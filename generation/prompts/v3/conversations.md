# Phase 1 — Social War Era (~91–87 BC): Conversation Map

## Player Start State
- **Rank:** Private Citizen (aspiring to Military Tribune)
- **Turns:** 30
- **Starting Dignitas:** Flat (all traits near zero)
- **Starting Factions:** Neutral across all
- **Starting Patronage:** 0 slots filled, 2 available
- **Disclosure Layer:** 0 (ambient knowledge only)

## Power Shift: Social War → Sullan March on Rome
**Outcome roll factors:** Faction alignment with whichever side wins (Sulla), survival of patron/allies, personal military record during the war.

## Conversation Map

### Act 1: Orientation (Conversations 1–10, ~Turns 1–10)

**Goal:** Establish the player's initial factional leanings, introduce the NPC roster, deliver Layer 0–1 context, offer first patronage opportunity.

**CONV-01 | Aurelia — "A Mother's Counsel"**
- **Direction:** Inbound (free)
- **Sequence:** 1 of 3
- **Turns:** 6 (1 passive, 4 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 0 (family position, cursus basics, the political landscape)
- **Plot function:** Cold open. Esablishes the player's family — respectable but not powerful, old connections to both sides. Aurelia warns Rome is more dangerous than it's been in a generation.
- **Key active choices:**
  - How the player describes ambitions (military / political / wealth → Audacia / Severitas / Calliditas nudge)
  - Interest in reform or tradition (first factional signal)
- **Exit states:**
  - *Dutiful son:* +1 Severitas. Unlocks Catulus (CONV-03) early.
  - *Ambitious upstart:* +1 Audacia. Unlocks Norbanus (CONV-04) early.
  - *Shrewd operator:* +1 Calliditas. Unlocks Atticus (CONV-05) early.

**CONV-02 | Scaurus — "The Weight of Custom"**
- **Direction:** Outbound (1 turn)
- **Sequence:** 1 of 2
- **Turns:** 7 (2 passive, 3 active, 2 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 1 → 2 (constitutional system, mos maiorum, why the Senate fears change)
- **Plot function:** Audience with the Senate's elder statesman. Scaurus monologues about the ancestral constitution. Player's responses determine whether Scaurus sees them as a worthy inheritor.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Agree with traditionalism (Optimates +1) or push back respectfully (neutral but earns respect)
  - Ask about Drusus's reforms (Layer 2 unlock: tribal voting math, not principle, drives citizenship opposition)
- **Exit states:**
  - *Traditionalist disciple:* Optimates +2, introduction to Catulus. Commitment: conservative signal — Marians hear about it.
  - *Respectful skeptic:* Optimates +1, Layer 2 constitutional content unlocked.
  - *Dismissed:* Scaurus finds you unserious. Locks CONV-08.

### Act 2: Escalation (Conversations 11–20, ~Turns 11–20)

**Goal:** Deepen factional commitments, introduce Sulla and Marius directly, deliver Layer 2 content, create commitment tensions. The Social War is now raging. Military service becomes relevant.

---

**CONV-11 | Atticus — "War Profiteering"**
- **Direction:** Outbound (1 turn)
- **Sequence:** 2 of 3
- **Turns:** 5 (1 passive, 3 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 (wartime economy, how conflict creates financial opportunity, eastern exposure crisis)
- **Plot function:** Atticus explains how the war is reshaping the financial landscape. Confiscated Italian property. Disrupted trade routes. Desperate senators selling assets. He offers the player a chance to profit — or to help stabilize allies' finances.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Accept Atticus's investment opportunity (Wealth +2 for the phase. Commitment: you now have financial exposure that needs the war to end in a specific way)
  - Use the information to help your faction (share intelligence with Catulus or Sulpicius — strengthens that patron relationship but Atticus notices)
  - Calliditas roll: negotiate a cut of Atticus's eastern contracts — high reward, high exposure
- **Exit states:**
  - *Business partner:* Wealth +2, Calliditas +1. Atticus patronage deepens. Commitment: financial interests now constrain your faction choices.
  - *Factional informant:* Patron relationship +2 (whichever patron you fed info to). Atticus trust -1.
  - *Cautious:* No financial benefit but no exposure either.

**CONV-12 | Catulus — "The Senate Mobilizes"**
- **Direction:** Outbound (1 turn) — requires Catulus patronage OR Optimates ≥ 3
- **Sequence:** 2 of 3
- **Turns:** 6 (2 passive, 3 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 (Senate's military strategy, factional jockeying over commands, the Sulla vs. Marius rivalry for eastern command)

- **Exit states:**
  - *Sulla's advocate:* Optimates +2, Sulla becomes aware of you (unlocks CONV-16). Commitment: Marians hear about your advocacy. Marian standing -2.
  - *Senate loyalist:* Optimates +1, no Marian penalty but no Sulla connection.
  - *Fence-sitter:* Catulus patronage weakens. -1 to patronage bonus until re-earned.

**CONV-13 | Poppaedius Silo — "The Enemy's Perspective" (CONDITIONAL)**
- **Direction:** Outbound (1 turn) — requires CONV-06 exit = reform ally OR CONV-07 exit = trusted intermediary
- **Sequence:** 1 of 2
- **Turns:** 7 (1 passive, 5 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 → 3 (Italian military capabilities, the allied war aims, what Rome looks like from the outside)
- **Plot function:** CRITICAL PERSPECTIVE SHIFT. Meeting an enemy commander during a truce or through back channels. Poppaedius is dignified, furious, and articulate. He explains why the Italians revolted and what they actually want. This conversation inverts the Roman-centric framing of all prior conversations.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Listen and ask genuine questions (Clementia +2. Layer 3: the Roman system has no enforcement mechanism — the Italians are proving it. If Rome's own allies can field armies against it, the entire model of Italian subordination is a fiction maintained by habit.)
  - Negotiate (Calliditas roll: what would it take to end the war? Poppaedius gives real terms. This information is extraordinarily valuable to either faction.)
  - Audacia-gated: offer your own services as a negotiation channel — dangerous, potentially treasonous, but could end the war faster
  - Argue Rome's case (Severitas +1, Poppaedius respects the conviction but tells you Romans will lose)
- **Exit states:**
  - *Trusted channel:* Italian Allies +3, Clementia +2. You now have a unique asset — access to both sides. This is enormously valuable at the power shift. But if either side discovers the extent of your contact, you're destroyed.
  - *Informed opponent:* Italian Allies +1. You understand their position without being compromised. Layer 3 content on constitutional enforcement unlocked.
  - *Blown cover:* If Audacia option fails, or if you shared Calliditas intelligence carelessly, your own faction discovers your contact. Major factional penalty. Turn penalty (3 turns — suspicion, investigation).

**CONV-14 | Marius — "The Old Lion" (CONDITIONAL)**
- **Direction:** Inbound (free) — triggers if Marians ≥ 4 OR CONV-09 exit = Marian operative
- **Sequence:** 1 of 2
- **Turns:** 6 (3 passive, 2 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 → 3 (Marius's view of the Republic, his army reform philosophy, his paranoia)
- **Plot function:** Marius summons you. This is NOT a pleasant conversation. Marius is aging, bitter, and erratic. He oscillates between brilliant strategic insight and paranoid rambling. The high passive count reflects that Marius dominates the conversation — the player mostly listens and reacts. The passive turns reveal the player's character to Marius based on their Dignitas profile.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Only two active choices, both consequential:
    1. When Marius asks if you'd follow him against the Senate itself: yes (Audacia +2, Marians +3. MAJOR commitment — you've pledged loyalty to a potential tyrant) or hedge (Calliditas +1, Marius becomes suspicious)
    2. When Marius reveals his plan to seize the eastern command from Sulla: support it (Marians +2, Optimates -3) or express concern (Clementia +1, Marius notes your hesitation)
- **Passive turns:** These are the most important in the game so far. Marius is reading you.
  - High Severitas: player responds with constitutional language → Marius snorts with contempt
  - High Audacia: player matches Marius's intensity → Marius is pleased but this locks you deeper
  - High Calliditas: player gives careful non-answers → Marius becomes suspicious ("I know a fence-sitter when I see one")
  - High Clementia: player tries to calm Marius → he's briefly touched, then furious at the condescension
- **Exit states:**
  - *Marius's man:* Marians +3, Audacia +2. Layer 3: Marius explains the army loyalty problem from the inside — the soldiers follow him because he's the only one who fought for their land. The Senate created this monster by refusing reform. MAJOR commitment: if Sulla wins, you are on a proscription list.
  - *Tolerated:* Marians +1. Marius doesn't trust you but doesn't actively dislike you. The Marian path continues but without the inner circle access.
  - *Suspected:* If both active choices went against Marius, he dismisses you coldly. Marians -2. Sulpicius is instructed not to share further plans with you.

**CONV-16 | Sulla — "The Rising Star" (CONDITIONAL)**
- **Direction:** Outbound (1 turn) — requires CONV-12 exit = Sulla's advocate OR Optimates ≥ 5
- **Sequence:** 1 of 2
- **Turns:** 7 (2 passive, 4 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 → 3 (Sulla's worldview, the constitutional crisis as he sees it, the calculus of force)
- **Plot function:** CRITICAL NPC INTRODUCTION. Sulla is brilliant, charming, and terrifying. He speaks with absolute clarity about what's coming. He's the first NPC to articulate Layer 3 openly: the system is broken and someone will have to fix it by force. The question is who.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Express admiration for his military record (Audacia +1, Sulla +2 personal favor)
  - Ask about his plans for the eastern command (Sulla reveals Layer 3: whoever controls the eastern army controls Rome's future. The command isn't about Mithridates — it's about having 40,000 loyal veterans when the real fight comes.)
  - Severitas-gated: argue that constitutional process should determine the command — Sulla agrees in principle and explains that's exactly why he'll follow the Senate's decision. Then adds, quietly, that if the Senate's decision is overturned by a tribune, all bets are off.
  - Ask about patronage (roll: Severitas or Audacia check. Success: **Patronage offered — Sulla.** This is the most powerful and most dangerous patron in the game.)
- **Exit states:**
  - *Sulla's protégé:* **Patronage offered — Sulla.** +3 Force on rolls. Passive override: military and political contexts default to Audacia. Commitment: you are now bound to a man who will march on Rome. If Sulla wins, you ascend. If he loses, you die. Layer 3: the proscription mechanism explained — Sulla has already thought about how to permanently restructure power through targeted killing and property redistribution.
  - *Respected ally:* Optimates +2, Sulla +2 personal favor. No patronage but favorable position.
  - *Kept at distance:* Sulla is cordial but sees you as insufficiently committed. Optimates +1.

**CONV-17 | Sulpicius — "The Power Play"**
- **Direction:** Inbound (free) — triggers at turn ~18 regardless (phase event: Sulpicius moves to transfer eastern command from Sulla to Marius)
- **Sequence:** 2 of 2
- **Turns:** 5 (2 passive, 2 active, 1 noop)
- **Layer delivery:** Layer 2 (the legislative mechanism for transferring military commands, the constitutional crisis this creates)
- **Plot function:** Sulpicius informs the player — whether allied or not — that he's moving to transfer the eastern command to Marius through the popular assembly. This is the fuse that leads to Sulla's march on Rome. The player's response is consequential.
- **Key active choices:**
  - Support the transfer (Marians +2, Optimates -3. You've chosen a side in the final crisis.)
  - Oppose the transfer (Optimates +2, Marians -3. Same — you've chosen.)
  - Passive turns: reputation determines your reflexive reaction before you choose. High Audacia = excitement. High Severitas = constitutional horror. High Calliditas = calculating who wins. High Clementia = trying to broker peace.
- **Exit states:**
  - *Marian committed:* Locked into Marian faction for power shift. Excellent position if Marius wins. Death sentence if Sulla wins.
  - *Optimate committed:* Locked into Optimate faction. Excellent if Sulla wins. Dangerous if Marius holds Rome.
  - *Uncommitted:* If player somehow avoided both commitments, this is the worst position — both sides distrust you. Power shift roll has no factional bonus.

## Key Design Properties

**Drusus as the first test:** CONV-06 is the most important early conversation. It's time-limited, and missing it permanently reduces the player's access to the Italian perspective and Layer 2–3 content about constitutional enforcement. This teaches the player that opportunities expire and information has value — the core lesson of the turn economy.

**Patronage as the gating mechanism:** The three patron options (Catulus/Atticus/Sulla) each open different Layer 2–3 content and different passive override behavior. The player can only hold 2 patronages, so taking Catulus + Sulla is an all-in Optimate play while Atticus + anyone is a hedged play. The combinations create meaningfully different game experiences.

**Passive turn escalation:** Act 1 conversations average ~20% passive. Act 2 averages ~35%. Act 3 averages ~60%. The power shift sequence is ~70% passive. The player physically experiences their agency shrinking as their identity solidifies — the lock-in mechanic working as intended.

**Commitment accumulation:** By mid-Act 2, a committed player has 3–4 active commitments constraining their choices. By Act 3, some exit states are locked out by prior promises. The power shift forces resolution of any contradictory commitments — you can't have promised both Catulus and Sulpicius that you'd support their side of the command dispute.

**Turn economy tension:** ~14 outbound conversations available. Only ~16–19 outbound turns available (30 minus inbound events and power shift). The player CANNOT do everything. They must choose which relationships to invest in and which to let lapse. This scarcity is the game.
